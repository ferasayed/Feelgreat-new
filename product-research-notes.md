## Additional findings from pages 6-10

### Page 6 — Cleveland Clinic continuation + Type-II Diabetes trials
The booklet reports that the placebo group showed an **LDL increase of 2.4%** over the same period. It also says homocysteine fell from **9.8 mmol/l to 8.7** after eight weeks, and apolipoprotein B fell from **139 to 110 mg/dl**. The booklet concludes that the fiber portion of Bios Life was effective in improving cardiovascular risk markers beyond diet alone, though it also characterizes the effect size as modest in subjects eating a more normal diet. It says the study was published in **Metabolism** in 2002.[Page 6]

The page then introduces **Type-II Diabetes Trials** and says Bios Life mechanisms are designed not only to lower cholesterol but also to interfere with carbohydrate absorption in the GI tract. It describes a first study from **1999-2000** involving **15 patients** who took the fiber portion of Bios Life for **90 days** while investigators tracked HbA1c, glucose, cholesterol, blood pressure, and weight.[Page 6]

Reported results for that first diabetes study include a **17% reduction** in pre-prandial glucose, a **36% reduction** in post-meal total glucose concentration measured two hours after meals, a **12% drop** in total cholesterol, a **42% drop** in triglycerides, a **6% increase** in HDL, and an average blood-pressure change from **145/82 mmHg to 131/77 mmHg**.[Page 6]

### Page 7 — Type-II Diabetes continuation + Philippines trial
The booklet says the drop in glucose absorption was associated with an HbA1c change from **9.2% to 7.8%**, presenting this as meaningful for long-term diabetes complication risk. It states these results were published in **Diabetes in Control** in 2000.[Page 7]

A second diabetes study is described with **78 type-II diabetics** over another 90-day period. According to the booklet, average glucose fell from **278 mg/dl to 237 mg/dl**, equivalent to an **HbA1c reduction of 10%**. It also says **LDL dropped 28.7%** and **HDL increased 27.9%**. The booklet notes that this study was presented at the **6th Annual Conference on Arteriosclerosis, Thrombosis and Vascular Biology** in Washington, D.C., and at the **65th Scientific Sessions of the American Diabetes Association** in San Diego in 2005, and that the American Heart Association selected it for press release distribution.[Page 7]

The page then introduces the **Philippines Trial** performed by **Dr. Norberto Yumol, M.D.** at the Angeles Foundation Medical Center in Angeles City, Philippines. It says **103 subjects** completed a four-week intervention, around **22%** of whom were diabetic and some of whom had prior myocardial infarction. The booklet reports a **15.8% reduction in total cholesterol** and says subjects moved from higher-risk LDL categories toward more desirable LDL groups.[Page 7]

### Page 8 — Philippines conclusion + Utah trial
The booklet adds that the effect of Bios Life in the Philippines study was more dramatic in the younger population aged **65 years or younger**, and says this supports beginning use earlier in life when risk-reduction effect is greatest. It notes presentation at the **34th Annual Convention of the Philippine College of Physicians**.[Page 8]

The same page introduces the **Utah Trial** on **Bios Life Complete** with its four cholesterol-lowering mechanisms. It says the first of these trials was performed in **Orem, Utah** on a general population sample with average baseline LDL of **131 mg/dl**. After eight weeks using **two packets per day**, **25 subjects** completed the protocol. The reported mean LDL reduction was **4.8%**, while among responders the average LDL reduction was **24.7%**. The booklet says **72%** of subjects were responders.[Page 8]

### Page 9 — Utah continuation + Pacific Rim trial
For Utah, the booklet says higher baseline LDL predicted better outcomes after eight weeks, and among subjects with baseline LDL of at least **160 mg/dl**, cholesterol fell **30.6%**. It also says that for responding subjects with baseline HDL lower than **40 mg/dl**, the intervention yielded a **28.6% reduction** in the baseline LDL-to-HDL relationship and a **24% drop** in cardiovascular risk ratio for those starting above a ratio of 5. The booklet notes the results were being considered for peer-reviewed publication and were not yet public at the time.[Page 9]

The page then presents the **Pacific Rim Trial** by **Dr. Vincent Duenas**, including both statin users and non-users. Among **15 statin users** with baseline LDL around **130 mg/dl** or higher, the booklet reports that after 12 weeks LDL fell **23.3%** and HDL rose **11.1%**. It argues this suggests an additive effect when Bios Life is used alongside statin therapy. The booklet also states that statins are not known to raise HDL, making the HDL increase noteworthy in its framing.[Page 9]

### Page 10 — Bios Life Slim glycemic-index study
This page presents a study titled **"The effects of a fiber-rich nutritional supplement, Bios Life Slim, on the glycemic index of three starchy foods"**. The background section frames glycemic control as important for health and says repeated high-glycemic surges correlate with higher risks of diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and obesity.[Page 10]

The objective is described as quantifying the impact of a fiber-rich nutritional supplement on the glycemic index of **white bread, white rice, and instant mashed potatoes**. The design involved **lean, healthy subjects (n=10)** consuming 50 grams of glucose in water and the three starchy foods, plus **7.25 g** or **14.5 g** of Bios Life Slim dissolved in **250 ml of water**. Standard glycemic and insulinemic index methods were used, with areas under the curve calculated for glucose and insulin.[Page 10]

Reported results:
- **One dose** of Bios Life Slim reduced glycemic index of white rice, instant mashed potatoes, white bread, and the combined average by **16.0%**, **16.9%**, **19.0%**, and **20.5%** respectively.
- **Two doses** reduced glycemic index of instant mashed potatoes, white rice, and white bread by **20.4%**, **24.7%**, and **27.7%** respectively.
- **One dose** reduced the insulinemic index of instant mashed potatoes, white rice, and white bread by **10.4%**, **16.9%**, and **21.3%** respectively.
- **Two doses** reduced the insulinemic index of instant mashed potatoes, white rice, and white bread by **15.7%**, **27.0%**, and **27.3%** respectively.[Page 10]

### Key conclusions from the full paper
- Bios Life Slim decreased the glycemic index of three common starchy foods in a dose-dependent manner
- In two of the three cases (white bread and white rice), it reduced their glycemic indices from High GI to Medium GI
- Bios Life Slim was most effective in reducing the glycemic and insulinemic indices of white bread and white rice
- Further research is needed to measure the effects of supplementation long-term
- Additional research is needed to measure the effects of Bios Life Slim with a population that is insulin-resistant or has Type-2 Diabetes
