## Study 1: Unimate (Yerba Mate) - GLP-1 and Appetite Suppression

### Source Data:
- **Title (Original):** "The Incretin Effect of Yerba Maté (Ilex paraguariensis) Is Partially Dependent on Gut-Mediated Metabolism of Ferulic Acid"
- **Journal:** Nutrients (MDPI)
- **DOI:** 10.3390/nu17040625
- **PubMed ID:** 40004954
- **PMC:** PMC11858593
- **Published:** February 9, 2025
- **Authors:** Cooper-Leavitt ET, Shin MJ, Beus CG, Chiu AT, Parker G, Radford JH, Evans EP, Edwards IT, Arroyo JA, Reynolds PR, Bikman BT
- **University:** Brigham Young University
- **Study Type:** Preclinical (in vivo + in vitro)
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate (preclinical but highly relevant)
- **Participant Count:** 12 mice (6 per group) + cell studies
- **Is Human Study:** No (animal + cell)
- **Is Preliminary:** Yes

### Key Findings:
1. YM supplementation significantly increased GLP-1 gene expression and plasma GLP-1 levels vs controls
2. Dihydroferulic acid (gut metabolite of ferulic acid) stimulated GLP-1 secretion by >2.5x in L-cells
3. YM selectively upregulates GLP-1 pathways without affecting GIP
4. Gut microbiome mediates the incretin effect of yerba mate
5. Yerba mate provided by Unicity International

### Additional Unicity Science Data (from website):
- Unimate acutely suppresses appetite: increased fullness by 59% within 15 minutes, reduced caloric intake by 11%
- Unimate prevents weight gain, improves cellular energy production, enhances antioxidant levels
- Unimate improves outcomes indicative of happiness

### Feel Great Connection:
Unimate is the morning component of the Feel Great system. Its ability to naturally stimulate GLP-1 production mimics the mechanism of expensive GLP-1 receptor agonist medications (like semaglutide), but through natural gut microbiome pathways. This makes it a practical, accessible option for appetite control and metabolic health.

## Study 2: Balance (Bios Life) - Cholesterol and Cardiometabolic Health

### Source Data (from ClinicalResearchBinder):
- **Title (Original):** "LDL- and HDL-cholesterol optimization using phytonutrient combination therapy: first line intervention and adjunct therapy to statins"
- **Journal:** Presented at 1st Asian Preventive Cardiology and Cardiac Rehabilitation Conference 2006, Hong Kong
- **Source URL:** https://www.unicityscience.org/studies
- **Published:** 2006
- **Authors:** Peter J.E. Verdegem, Vincent Duenas, Julie Duenas, Evelyn Burke
- **University:** The Doctors Clinic, Tamuning, GU / Unicity International, Orem, UT
- **Study Type:** Multi-site clinical trial (3 studies)
- **Evidence Level:** Moderate
- **Participant Count:** 99 (Cleveland Clinic RCT data combined)
- **Is Human Study:** Yes
- **Is Preliminary:** No

### Key Findings (from ClinicalResearchBinder):
1. Study 1: LDL-c reduced from 168 to 127 mg/dL (-24.5%, p<0.0001)
2. Study 2 (adjunct to statins): LDL-c reduced from 150 to 118 (-21.1%, p<0.05), HDL-c increased from 34 to 42 (+23.2%, p<0.05)
3. Study 3: LDL-c reduced from 154 to 119 (-22.5%, p<0.05)
4. Product works through 4 different mechanisms: viscous soluble fiber, policosanol, phytosterols, Chrysanthemum morifolium
5. Effective both as first-line treatment and adjunct to statins

### Additional Data (from ClinicalResearchBinder - Diabetes study):
- 34 subjects with type II diabetes: TC -14.2%, LDL-c -18.3%, TG -21.3%, Glucose -17.3%, HbA1c -9.4%
- In high-risk subgroups: LDL-c -28.9%, TG -32.5%, Glucose -28.9%, HbA1c -15.8%

### Feel Great Connection:
Balance is the evening component of Feel Great. Its fiber matrix creates a gel in the digestive tract that slows carbohydrate absorption, reduces cholesterol reabsorption, and feeds beneficial gut bacteria. The multi-mechanism approach addresses cardiovascular risk from multiple angles simultaneously.
