# Unicity Compliance Guidelines

## Internet Use and Social Media Guidelines for Promoting Feel Great

1. All websites/social media channels are subject to review by Unicity Compliance Team
2. URL/Domain Name must NOT be Unicity name(s), Trademark(s), Logo(s) or Product Name(s)
3. Allowed URL structures: "Feelgreatby____" or "Feelgreatwith____" (insert personal/account name)
4. Ads from Google, Instagram, or Facebook are NOT allowed (even free ads)
5. Selling on Amazon, eBay, Facebook marketplace etc. is NOT allowed
6. Website must state: "He/she is an Independent Unicity Distributor with Unicity ID"
7. Website must state: "This is the website of an Independent Unicity Distributor. For the official Unicity website, please refer to www.unicity.com"
8. Must display "Retail Price of the product"
9. Discounts are NOT allowed on website/social media
10. Must display Unicity Product Information from official site/publications only
11. Prescriptive, disease, medical, and/or therapeutic claims are PROHIBITED
12. Personal testimonials need to be first-hand and approved by Compliance Team
13. Must display Distributor's contact info (phone, WhatsApp, email)
14. Must NOT offer competing products
15. Must include Customer Refund Policy of 90 days
16. Must obtain consent before using other Distributors' posts/videos
17. Spamming mass emails/private conversations is PROHIBITED

## Social Media Guidelines & Ad Training (Compliance)

### Business Opportunity Claims - Key Rules:
1. **Policies & Procedures**: Violations can lead to disciplinary action of distributorship
2. **FTC Oversight**: Federal Trade Commission has oversight over ALL claims the company makes. They assign claims made by distributors TO the company - company is legally responsible
3. **Business Sustainability**: Must be honest and forthright about the opportunity. Business is NOT easy and requires hard work. Do NOT tell stories of easy money or miracle products. Over-promising leads to disappointment and turnover.

### Critical Compliance Rules for the Chatbot:
- NEVER guarantee income or specific earnings
- NEVER claim products cure/treat/prevent diseases
- Must be transparent that success requires work
- Must not make lifestyle claims implying guaranteed wealth
- All claims must be truthful and substantiated

### Core Principle: Do NOT make misleading claims

### Misleading Claims Definition:
It is misleading for a direct selling company and/or its salesforce members to make any earnings claims unless:
(A) have a reasonable basis for the claim at the time it is made
(B) have documentation that substantiates the claim at the time it is made

### What is a Business Opportunity Claim?
Any statement express or implied that conveys:
1. A range of potential earnings or profits
2. A representation of living a lavish lifestyle or earned company-sponsored incentives
3. A promise or guarantee of any financial outcome

### Earnings Claims Prohibited:
- (A) A level or range of actual or potential earnings that may be earned by distributors
- (B) A level or range of gross or net income or profits, including representations that suggest the ability to make lifestyle purchases (homes, vehicles, vacations) are related to income earned from direct selling

### CHATBOT COMPLIANCE RULES (MUST FOLLOW):
1. Never state specific dollar amounts of potential income
2. Never guarantee financial outcomes
3. Never show lavish lifestyle as result of joining
4. Never promise "easy money" or "passive income"
5. Always mention that results vary and require effort
6. Focus on product benefits (health) not income claims
7. Can mention "opportunity" but not specific earnings
8. Must include disclaimer that individual results vary
